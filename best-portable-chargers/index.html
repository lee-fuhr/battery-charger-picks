Whether you are stuck on a long flight, camping off-grid, or just find your phone dead by mid-afternoon, a portable power bank has become an essential carry. With so many on the market, the main trade-off comes down to capacity versus size and speed: bigger batteries mean more top-ups but also more weight in your bag, and fast-charging support (Power Delivery or Quick Charge) can cut your wait from hours to minutes. To pick the right one, focus on how you travel: a daily commuter can get away with a slim 10,000mAh pack, while a road-tripper or photographer will want 20,000mAh or more. Also check that the power bank matches your devices’ charging standards, if you own a recent iPhone or Android flagship, USB-C Power Delivery is the way to go.
