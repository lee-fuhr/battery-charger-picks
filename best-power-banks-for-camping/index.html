When you’re heading into the backcountry or setting up a basecamp, a dependable power source is no longer a luxury, it’s a necessity. Between navigating with GPS apps, capturing photos, running a headlamp at night, and keeping communication devices alive, your gear draws more power than a standard phone battery can handle. A rugged, high-capacity power bank (or a portable power station) can mean the difference between a comfortable trip and a stressful one. Choosing the right camping battery charger comes down to three things: capacity (how many full charges you get), output options (USB-A, USB-C, or AC outlets), and durability (weather resistance, build quality). You’ll also want to consider weight and how you plan to recharge the bank itself, solar readiness is a huge bonus for extended trips. Best for specific needs

Best for multi-day basecamp trips

When you’re stationary for several days with multiple devices and maybe a small cooler or an inflatable mattress pump, you need a power station that can handle AC loads. The Jackery Explorer 300 Plus offers a pure sine wave inverter and enough capacity to keep everything running without hunting for a wall outlet. Pair it with a foldable solar panel and you can stay off-grid indefinitely. Our pick: Jackery Explorer 300 Plus.

Best for lightweight backpacking and weekend hikes

If you’re covering miles on foot and just need to keep a phone, headlamp, and maybe a GPS unit alive, a compact power bank is the way to go. The Anker PowerCore 20,000mAh Portable Charger packs plenty of juice into a small, durable package that won’t weigh down your pack. Its efficiency and reliability make it a favorite among thru-hikers and weekend warriors alike. Our pick: Anker PowerCore 20,000mAh Portable Charger.

Best for large groups or extended car-camping trips on a budget

Just be prepared to wait overnight for the bank itself to recharge from a wall outlet. Our pick: RAVPower 30,000mAh Portable Charger.

Our verdict

No matter how you camp, from a basecamp with all the gear to a minimalist backpacking trip, having a dependable way to recharge your electronics makes the experience safer and more enjoyable. If you need AC power and plan to stay put, the Jackery Explorer 300 Plus is the clear winner. For the vast majority of campers who just want a reliable, high-capacity power bank, the Anker PowerCore 20,000mAh Portable Charger is our top overall recommendation. And if you’re on a tight budget but still need serious capacity, the RAVPower 30,000mAh Portable Charger delivers exceptional value for the price.
